So how effective are vaccinations?

Let’s take a closer look at our most recent outbreak, COVID-19!

There are many articles out there that aim to lead the readers astray.
For example, imagine a population of 60 people. 10 out of 60 passed away from the virus, and 50% of those who died were vaccinated.
A news headline may say,
“Half of those who died from COVID-19 were vaccinated!”
Seems accurate, right?

Wrong!

Visualise the bigger picture:

How many people in total were vaccinated?

Out of the entire population, 50 of them were vaccinated, leaving the other 10 unvaccinated. Now we see that although half of those who died were vaccinated, 90% of the vaccinated population did not die from the virus. On the other hand, only 50% of the unvaccinated did not die from COVID-19.

United States: COVID-19 weekly death rate by vaccination status, All ages

Death rates are calculated as the number of deaths in each group, divided by the total number of people in this group. This is given per 100,000 people.

COVID-19 Cases and Deaths by Vaccination Status in Malaysia

This chart presents the distribution of COVID-19 cases and associated fatalities among unvaccinated, partially vaccinated, and fully vaccinated individuals in Malaysia, highlighting the impact of vaccination on disease outcomes.


Data source: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, Vaccine Breakthrough/Surveillance and Analytics Team – Learn more about this data
Note: The mortality rate for the 'All ages' group is age-standardized to account for the different vaccination rates of older and younger people. OurWorldInData.org/coronavirus | CC BY

As you can see from this chart, throughout the COVID-19 pandemic, the mortality rate of the unvaccinated in The U.S. remained drastically higher than the vaccinated with or without bivalent booster (Mathieu & Roser, 2024).

Open data on COVID-19 in Malaysia – Learn more about this data

It was discovered that the total age-standardised mortality rates for the unvaccinated population in Malaysia were 43.2 times higher than the fully vaccinated and 12.5 times higher than the partially vaccinated (Abdul Taib et al., 2022). These numbers display the effectiveness of vaccinations and significance of immunisation.
